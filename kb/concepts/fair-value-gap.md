# Fair Value Gap (FVG) — Core Concept

## Definition

A Fair Value Gap (FVG) is a **3-candle formation** where the expansive middle candle creates a gap between the wicks of candles 1 and 3. This gap represents an **imbalance in price delivery** — price moved so aggressively that it left behind an inefficiency that the market will seek to rebalance. FVGs are the single most important concept in ICT methodology — they confirm displacement, provide entry zones, define bias, and act as the "internal range liquidity" that price cycles through.

> FVGs are to ICT what support/resistance is to traditional technical analysis. They are everywhere, on every timeframe, and form the backbone of every setup.

## Why FVGs Matter

1. **They confirm displacement** — An FVG forming in a move proves the move is impulsive, not corrective
2. **They define bias** — The direction of recent valid FVGs shows who is in control
3. **They provide entries** — Price retracing to fill an FVG is the primary entry mechanism
4. **They are IRL (Internal Range Liquidity)** — Price alternates between FVGs (IRL) and swing points (ERL)
5. **They create the BSG** — A Break Structure Gap (FVG at a structure break) is the lifeblood of any trend

## Formation Rules

### Bullish FVG
```
Candle 1:  ----[====]----    (any candle)
                              GAP: Candle 3 Low > Candle 1 High
Candle 2:       [=========]  (large bullish displacement candle)
                              
Candle 3:            [====]  (any candle)
```

- **Top of FVG** = Candle 3's Low
- **Bottom of FVG** = Candle 1's High
- **Gap** = the vertical space between Candle 1 High and Candle 3 Low
- Candle 2 is the displacement candle that creates the imbalance

### Bearish FVG
```
Candle 1:       [====]----   (any candle)
                              GAP: Candle 3 High < Candle 1 Low
Candle 2:  [=========]       (large bearish displacement candle)
                              
Candle 3:  ----[====]        (any candle)
```

- **Top of FVG** = Candle 1's Low
- **Bottom of FVG** = Candle 3's High
- **Gap** = the vertical space between Candle 3 High and Candle 1 Low

## Three Critical Levels Inside Every FVG

| Level | Name | Fibonacci | Significance |
|-------|------|-----------|-------------|
| **Open** | FVG Open / Nearest Edge | 0.0 | Where price first enters the FVG |
| **Midpoint** | Consequent Encroachment (CE) | 0.5 | The decision line — reactions here determine validity |
| **Fill** | FVG Fill / Far Edge | 1.0 | Complete rebalancing of the imbalance |

### Consequent Encroachment (CE)
The **50% level** of any FVG (or any gap — volume imbalance, NDOG, NWOG, even wicks). CE is the single most important level within an FVG. If price stays on one side of CE, the FVG is respected. If it crosses CE, the FVG's power is weakening or inverting.

## The Three FVG Reaction Rules

These rules determine whether an FVG is valid, invalid, or inverted — and thus determine your bias and next action:

### Rule 1: Valid FVG → Continuation Expected
- Price taps into the FVG
- Price **fails to close beyond the midpoint (CE / 0.5 level)**
- The FVG is **valid** — expect continuation in the original direction
- **Action**: Trade in the FVG's direction. Use the FVG as an entry zone.

### Rule 2: Invalid FVG → Reassess
- Price enters the FVG
- Price **closes beyond the midpoint** but does NOT close completely through
- The FVG is **weakened/invalid** — don't trust it
- **Action**: Wait for the next FVG to form. Do not trade off this one.

### Rule 3: Inversion → New Direction (iFVG)
- Price **closes completely through** the FVG from the opposite side
- The old FVG has been **inverted** — it flips polarity
- A bearish FVG that price closes up through → becomes bullish support (iFVG)
- A bullish FVG that price closes down through → becomes bearish resistance (iFVG)
- **Action**: Trade in the new direction. The iFVG is now your reference level.
- See also: [Inverse Fair Value Gap skill](../skills/inverse-fair-value-gap.md)

## FVG Types and Hierarchy

### Break Structure Gap (BSG)
The FVG that forms when market structure is broken. **BSGs are the most important FVGs** — they are the lifeblood of a trend.

- BSG must follow the **consistency rule** (one-sided delivery — all candles in the same direction)
- If a BSG fails (opposing candle closes through it) → look for the opposing swing point as target
- A failed BSG inverts and becomes a level in the new direction
- **BSG + Inflection Point = highest probability level** in the market

### Inflection Points
When a swing high/low is broken, extend that level horizontally across the chart. Where this extended level intersects with a BSG = **inflection point**. Expect strong displacement away from inflection points.

### Volume Imbalance
A special type of gap that occurs at the boundary of an FVG — when the wick of candle 1 and the wick of candle 3 touch but only at the extreme. The CE of a volume imbalance is significant.

### New Day/Week Opening Gap (NDOG / NWOG)
- **NDOG**: Gap between 16:59 close and 18:00 open. Lifespan: 5 days.
- **NWOG**: Gap between Friday 16:59 close and Sunday 18:00 open. Lifespan: 5 weeks.
- Both act as strong draws on liquidity with their own CE (50% level).

### Wicks as Gaps
Long wicks (especially news-driven wicks) can function as FVGs. The CE of a wick is significant. Price often reacts at the 50% level of a long wick.

## FVG Quality Filters

| Quality Factor | High Probability | Low Probability |
|---------------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Count per leg** | Single, clean FVG | Multiple FVGs in same leg |
| **Context** | Forms after a liquidity sweep | Random / no context |
| **Alignment** | In premium (for sells) or discount (for buys) | At equilibrium |
| **Displacement** | Large-body candle 2 with small wicks | Small-body candle 2 |
| **Delivery** | One-sided (all candles same direction) | Two-sided (mixed) |
| **Timeframe** | HTF FVGs (Daily, H4) | Very LTF FVGs (M1 in isolation) |
| **At BSG** | FVG is a Break Structure Gap | Regular FVG |
| **First in leg** | First FVG after structure break or 9:30 | Fourth or fifth FVG in a leg |

### Special: First FVG After 9:30 AM EST
The first FVG that forms after the 9:30 AM market open is particularly significant. It often serves as key support/resistance for the entire trading day. The first FVG before a stop hunt acts as a Change in State of Delivery.

## Uses of FVGs

| Use | How |
|-----|-----|
| **HTF Key Levels** | Daily/H4 FVGs act as support/resistance zones on LTF |
| **Directional Bias** | Valid FVGs = continuation. Invalid/inverted = reassess. |
| **Trade Entries** | Enter on retracement to FVG (limit at FVG open or midpoint) |
| **Stop Losses** | Place stops beyond the FVG fill level |
| **Draw on Liquidity (IRL)** | Unfilled FVGs ahead of price serve as targets |
| **Confirm Displacement** | FVG forming in a move = displacement = genuine |
| **Daily Bias** | Most recent daily FVG tap + reaction rules = daily direction |

## Agent Detection Logic — FVG Scanner

```python
class FVG:
    """Represents a Fair Value Gap."""
    def __init__(self, top, bottom, direction, candle_index, timestamp,
                 candle_1, candle_2, candle_3):
        self.top = top          # Upper boundary
        self.bottom = bottom    # Lower boundary
        self.midpoint = (top + bottom) / 2  # CE / Consequent Encroachment
        self.direction = direction  # BULLISH or BEARISH
        self.candle_index = candle_index
        self.timestamp = timestamp
        self.size = top - bottom
        self.candle_1 = candle_1
        self.candle_2 = candle_2
        self.candle_3 = candle_3
        self.status = "UNFILLED"  # UNFILLED, VALID, INVALID, INVERTED


def detect_all_fvgs(candles):
    """
    Scan an array of candles and return all Fair Value Gaps.
    Each candle must have: open, high, low, close, timestamp.
    """
    fvgs = []
    
    for i in range(1, len(candles) - 1):
        c1 = candles[i - 1]
        c2 = candles[i]       # The displacement candle
        c3 = candles[i + 1]
        
        # BULLISH FVG: Candle 3's low is ABOVE Candle 1's high
        if c3.low > c1.high:
            fvg = FVG(
                top=c3.low,       # Upper boundary = C3 low
                bottom=c1.high,   # Lower boundary = C1 high
                direction="BULLISH",
                candle_index=i,
                timestamp=c2.timestamp,
                candle_1=c1, candle_2=c2, candle_3=c3
            )
            fvgs.append(fvg)
        
        # BEARISH FVG: Candle 3's high is BELOW Candle 1's low
        if c3.high < c1.low:
            fvg = FVG(
                top=c1.low,       # Upper boundary = C1 low
                bottom=c3.high,   # Lower boundary = C3 high
                direction="BEARISH",
                candle_index=i,
                timestamp=c2.timestamp,
                candle_1=c1, candle_2=c2, candle_3=c3
            )
            fvgs.append(fvg)
    
    return fvgs


def evaluate_fvg_status(fvg, subsequent_candles):
    """
    Evaluate an FVG's status by checking how subsequent candles interact with it.
    Returns: VALID, INVALID, INVERTED, or UNFILLED.
    """
    for candle in subsequent_candles:
        if fvg.direction == "BULLISH":
            # Price entered the FVG from above (retracing down)
            if candle.low <= fvg.top:  # Price touched the FVG
                if candle.close > fvg.midpoint:
                    # Rule 1: Failed to close below midpoint → VALID
                    return "VALID"
                elif candle.close <= fvg.midpoint and candle.close > fvg.bottom:
                    # Rule 2: Closed below midpoint but not through → INVALID
                    return "INVALID"
                elif candle.close <= fvg.bottom:
                    # Rule 3: Closed completely through → INVERTED (now bearish iFVG)
                    return "INVERTED"
        
        elif fvg.direction == "BEARISH":
            # Price entered the FVG from below (retracing up)
            if candle.high >= fvg.bottom:  # Price touched the FVG
                if candle.close < fvg.midpoint:
                    return "VALID"
                elif candle.close >= fvg.midpoint and candle.close < fvg.top:
                    return "INVALID"
                elif candle.close >= fvg.top:
                    return "INVERTED"
    
    return "UNFILLED"


def detect_bsg(candles, swing_points, fvgs):
    """
    Detect Break Structure Gaps — FVGs that form at the exact point 
    where market structure is broken.
    """
    bsgs = []
    
    for fvg in fvgs:
        for swing in swing_points:
            if fvg.direction == "BULLISH":
                # Bullish FVG that breaks above a swing high
                if fvg.bottom >= swing.price and swing.type == "HIGH":
                    bsgs.append({
                        "fvg": fvg,
                        "broken_structure": swing,
                        "type": "BULLISH_BSG",
                        "inflection_point": swing.price  # Extend this level forward
                    })
            
            elif fvg.direction == "BEARISH":
                # Bearish FVG that breaks below a swing low
                if fvg.top <= swing.price and swing.type == "LOW":
                    bsgs.append({
                        "fvg": fvg,
                        "broken_structure": swing,
                        "type": "BEARISH_BSG",
                        "inflection_point": swing.price
                    })
    
    return bsgs


def score_fvg_quality(fvg, candles, context):
    """
    Score an FVG from 0-10 for trade quality.
    Higher score = higher probability of being respected.
    """
    score = 0
    
    # 1. Displacement strength (candle 2 body ratio)
    c2 = fvg.candle_2
    body = abs(c2.close - c2.open)
    total_range = c2.high - c2.low
    body_ratio = body / total_range if total_range > 0 else 0
    if body_ratio > 0.7:
        score += 2  # Strong displacement
    elif body_ratio > 0.5:
        score += 1
    
    # 2. Single FVG in the leg (not one of many)
    nearby_fvgs = count_fvgs_in_leg(candles, fvg, lookback=10)
    if nearby_fvgs == 1:
        score += 2  # Clean, singular
    elif nearby_fvgs <= 2:
        score += 1
    
    # 3. Context: after liquidity sweep
    if context.get("after_sweep"):
        score += 2
    
    # 4. Is it a BSG?
    if context.get("is_bsg"):
        score += 2
    
    # 5. In premium/discount alignment
    if context.get("in_pd_zone"):
        score += 1
    
    # 6. First FVG after 9:30 or after structure break
    if context.get("is_first_fvg"):
        score += 1
    
    return score  # Max 10. Trade if >= 6.


def detect_ndog(candles, date):
    """
    Detect New Day Opening Gap.
    NDOG = gap between 16:59 close and 18:00 open.
    """
    close_1659 = get_price_at(candles, date, time(16, 59))
    open_1800 = get_price_at(candles, date, time(18, 0))
    
    if close_1659 and open_1800:
        return {
            "high": max(close_1659, open_1800),
            "low": min(close_1659, open_1800),
            "ce": (close_1659 + open_1800) / 2,
            "lifespan_days": 5
        }
    return None


def detect_nwog(candles, friday_date):
    """
    Detect New Week Opening Gap.
    NWOG = gap between Friday 16:59 close and Sunday 18:00 open.
    """
    friday_close = get_price_at(candles, friday_date, time(16, 59))
    sunday_open = get_price_at(candles, friday_date + timedelta(days=2), time(18, 0))
    
    if friday_close and sunday_open:
        return {
            "high": max(friday_close, sunday_open),
            "low": min(friday_close, sunday_open),
            "ce": (friday_close + sunday_open) / 2,
            "lifespan_weeks": 5
        }
    return None
```

## Relationship to Other Concepts

| Concept | Relationship to FVG |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Displacement** | FVGs CONFIRM displacement. No FVG = no displacement = manipulation. |
| **Order Blocks** | Displacement away from an OB often leaves an FVG behind. |
| **Breaker Blocks** | Breaker + FVG overlap = [Unicorn Model](../strategies/unicorn-model.md) entry |
| **Liquidity** | FVGs ARE Internal Range Liquidity (IRL). Price alternates between FVGs and swing points. |
| **Premium/Discount** | Apply 0/0.5/1 Fibonacci to FVGs just like to impulse legs. |
| **Market Structure** | BSG = the FVG at a structure break = the trend's lifeblood. |
| **Daily Bias** | Most recent daily FVG reaction (3 rules) = daily directional bias. |
| **Silver Bullet** | Entry is the first FVG in the 10:00-11:00 or 14:00-15:00 window. |
