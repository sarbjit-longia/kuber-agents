# Risk Management

## Universal Risk Principles

These rules apply across ALL strategies in this knowledge base.

## Position Sizing

### R-Value System

**R = Risk per trade as a percentage of account equity**

| Account State | Risk Per Trade |
|--------------|---------------|
| At breakeven (equity = starting capital) | **1R** (standard risk) |
| 2R or more in profit | **2R** (increased risk — only when meaningfully ahead) |
| In drawdown | **0.5R** (reduced risk — preserve capital) |

### Recommended R Values
| Trader Level | R Value |
|-------------|---------|
| Beginner | 0.5-1% of account |
| Intermediate | 1-2% of account |
| Advanced | 2% maximum |

**Never risk more than 2% of account equity on a single trade.**

## Minimum Reward-to-Risk Ratios

| Strategy | Minimum R:R |
|----------|------------|
| 3-Step Liquidity System | 2:1 |
| 6-Figure ICT Strategy | 3:1 |
| Silver Bullet | Fixed 2:1 |
| Range Expansion | 2:1+ (often 5:1+) |
| SMM Entry Model | 2:1+ |
| Unicorn Model | 2:1+ |
| Credit Spreads | Defined at entry |

### R:R Math
Even with a 40% win rate, a 3:1 R:R strategy is profitable:
- 10 trades: 4 wins × 3R = 12R, 6 losses × 1R = 6R → Net = +6R

## Stop Loss Rules

### Hard Rules
1. **Always use a hard stop loss** — never mental stops
2. Place the stop at **order entry** and walk away
3. Stop placement should be **logical** (beyond a structural level), not arbitrary

### Strategy-Specific Stop Placement

| Strategy | Stop Placement |
|----------|---------------|
| ICT entries (MSS/FVG) | Beyond the swing created by the manipulation |
| Order Block entry | Below/above the OB extreme |
| Breaker Block entry | Beyond the breaker candle extreme |
| Manipulation Block | At the MB's low (long) or high (short) |
| Silver Bullet | Beyond candle 1 of the FVG formation |
| Harmonic patterns | Beyond point X (Gartley/Bat) or beyond D (Butterfly/Crab) |
| Classic patterns | On the opposite side of the pattern |

### What NOT To Do
- **Do NOT place stops just below support** — this is where liquidity clusters and stops get hunted
- Use **ATR-based buffers** instead (1 ATR below the structural level)

## Daily Trading Rules

### ICT-Style Day Trading

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **2 losses = done for the day** | No more trades after 2 consecutive losses |
| **1 win = done for the day** | Lock in profits — prevents giving back gains (6-Figure strategy) |
| **Maximum trades** | 2 per day (Silver Bullet: 1 per window, 2 windows) |

### Options/Swing Trading

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **1-2% risk per trade** | 0.25-0.5% ideal (TastyTrade) |
| **50% cash minimum** | Never be fully invested |
| **Exit winners at 50%** | Close at 50% of max profit (options) |
| **Exit losers at 2x credit** | Close when loss = 2× the credit received |

## Taking Profits

### Trimming vs Breakeven

| Method | When to Use | Pro | Con |
|--------|------------|-----|-----|
| **Trimming (partial profits)** | When first target is hit | Locks in some profit, lets rest run | Smaller total if trade goes to full target |
| **Breakeven stop** | After first internal liquidity hit + no displacement | Eliminates risk | Often gets stopped out then runs |
| **No modification** | 6-Figure strategy | Lets trade fully play out | Full risk maintained |

**General preference**: Trimming > Breakeven > No modification

### Partial Profit Rules (ICT 2024)
- Take a partial at **every short-term high** that is broken (when long)
- Do NOT move your stop loss after partials
- Even with a higher target in mind, it does NOT have to reach that level in your specific trade

## Trading Psychology Rules

1. **Trading is 90% emotion, 10% execution** — master your emotions
2. **Accept losses as part of the business** — a loss is not a failure, it's a cost of doing business
3. **Be mechanical** — follow rules, not feelings
4. **Consistency is key** — consistent with trades, consistent with yourself
5. **Never risk money you cannot afford to lose**
6. **Start small and build slowly**
7. **If you can paint both bullish and bearish scenarios easily** → Low probability, no trade

## Pre-Trade Checklist

Before EVERY trade, confirm:

- [ ] Clear directional bias (HTF analysis)
- [ ] Appropriate timing (killzone / session)
- [ ] Valid entry model (MSS, FVG, pattern, etc.)
- [ ] Logical stop loss placement (structural level)
- [ ] Minimum R:R achieved (2:1 or strategy-specific minimum)
- [ ] Position size within risk limits (1-2% max)
- [ ] Daily trade limit not exceeded
- [ ] No conflicting news events imminent
